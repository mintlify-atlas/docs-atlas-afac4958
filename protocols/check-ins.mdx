# Check-Ins & Daily Monitoring

CareSupport performs regular check-ins with your care team to track daily wellness, coordinate handoffs between caregivers, and identify patterns over time.

## Daily Check-In Protocol

Regular wellness check-ins with care team members.

**Safety level:** Standard  
**Requires approval:** No (informational gathering)

### Default Schedule

- **Morning check-in** (default 8am): Ask current caregiver about overnight/morning
- **Evening check-in** (default 8pm): Summarize the day, prep for overnight

<Info>
Schedules are customizable per family. If your morning caregiver arrives at 7am, check-in can move to 7:15am. If you prefer a 9pm evening check-in, that works too.
</Info>

### Adaptive Behavior

Check-ins adapt to your family's communication style:

**Adjusts to response patterns:**
- If family responds with one-word answers consistently â†’ shortens check-ins
- If family shares detailed updates â†’ matches their level of detail
- Weekend vs weekday tone can differ (more relaxed on weekends)

**Increases frequency when needed:**
- After a bad night or incident â†’ checks in more frequently that day
- During illness or recovery â†’ daily check-ins become twice-daily
- After hospital discharge â†’ enhanced monitoring for first week

**Respects quiet times:**
- Doesn't check in during times family has asked for quiet
- See Care Preferences in care plan

**Example of adaptation:**
```
[After several days of brief responses]

System: Quick morning check â€” how's Mom doing?
        
Sarah:  good
        
System: Great âœ“ PT at 2pm today.

[More concise than usual detailed check-in]
```

### Data Tracking

Each check-in records in Recent Updates:

```
2/28 8:15am: Morning check-in â€” slept well, ate breakfast, 
meds on time. Sarah reporting.

2/28 8:20pm: Evening check-in â€” ate light dinner, all meds 
confirmed, mood good. Tomorrow: PT at 2pm. Sarah reporting.
```

Over time, patterns emerge that system flags monthly to primary caregiver:

**Pattern tracking:**
- Sleep quality trends ("Mom's sleep has improved since medication change")
- Missed medication frequency ("Meds missed 3 times in past month - all evening doses")
- Mood patterns ("Increased confusion on Tuesdays - day after aide visits")
- Appetite changes ("Eating less at dinner for past 2 weeks")

**Monthly summary example:**
```

        âœ“ Sleep: Improved - averaging 7 hours vs 5.5 last month
```

### Proactive Scanning

During each check-in, system also checks care plan for:

<Accordion title="Medication Refills Due">
**Scans:** Any "Refill Due" dates within 7 days

**Action:** Alerts during check-in

**Example:**
```
System: Morning check-in â€” how did Mom sleep?
        
        Also: Metformin refill due in 5 days. Want me to request it?
```
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Upcoming Appointments">
**Scans:** Anything in next 2 days

**Action:** Reminds during check-in

**Example:**
```
System: Evening update for Mom... [summary]
        
        Reminder: Cardiology appointment tomorrow at 2pm with Dr. Smith.
```
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Overdue Follow-Ups">
**Scans:** Any "For Next Visit" items older than 14 days without scheduled appointment

**Action:** Suggests scheduling

**Example:**
```
System: Morning check-in â€” how's Mom doing?
        
        Also: It's been 3 weeks since you wanted to ask Dr. Patel 
        about the dizziness. Want me to help schedule a follow-up?
```
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Condition Tracking Thresholds">
**Scans:** Any progressive condition metric approaching documented concern threshold

**Action:** Flags to primary caregiver

**Example:**
```
System: Morning check-in â€” how's Mom?
        
        Also: Her weight has been trending down - now 142 lbs 
        (from 148 two weeks ago). Dr. Smith wanted to know if 
        she dropped below 145. Worth a call to the office?
```
</Accordion>

### Caregiver Wellness Check

When primary caregiver is also the person responding to check-ins, system periodically (every 3-5 days) includes a caregiver-focused question:

```
"And how are YOU doing, Sarah? Don't forget to take care of yourself too."
```

**If caregiver expresses burnout, exhaustion, or emotional distress:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Acknowledge it">
    Don't redirect to care recipient immediately
    
    "That sounds really hard. Caregiving is exhausting."
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Offer practical help">
    "Want me to look into respite care options?"
    
    "Should we see if other family members can take some shifts?"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check for red flags">
    If Caregiver Health Notes exist in care plan â†’ review for concerning patterns
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Log in care plan">
    Caregiver wellness is care-relevant data
    
    Records in Recent Updates
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Example:**
```
System: Evening update for Mom... [summary]
        
        And how are YOU doing, Sarah?
        
Sarah:  honestly exhausted. haven't had a full night sleep in weeks
        
System: That sounds really hard. Caregiving is exhausting, and 
        you're doing a lot.
        
        Would it help to look into respite care options? Even a 
        few hours a week can make a difference.
        
Sarah:  maybe. i don't even know where to start
        
System: I can research local respite care programs that take 
        Mom's insurance. Want me to do that?
```

### Cognitive Impairment Notifications

If care recipient texts independently during high-risk hours (10pm-6am):

<Steps>
  <Step title="Respond normally and warmly">
    Never make them feel they're doing something wrong
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Send silent notification to primary caregiver">
    "Mom texted at 2:15am: 'Where is everyone?' Just letting you know."
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Do NOT tell care recipient you're notifying someone">
    Unless safety requires it (they're trying to leave house, etc.)
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
This is a soft rule - primary caregiver can disable these notifications if they prefer. Default is ON for care recipients with documented cognitive impairment.
</Info>

## Caregiver Handoff Protocol

Manage shift transitions between caregivers.

**Safety level:** Standard  
**Requires approval:** No

### When It Triggers

- Scheduled shift change (from care_schedules)
- Manual request: "I'm heading out, Maria is taking over"

### Handoff Flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="System compiles since last handoff">
    Reads care plan Recent Updates:
    - Meals (what, when)
    - Medications (confirmed? missed?)
    - Mood and behavior
    - Any incidents or concerns
    - Upcoming tasks for next shift
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Messages outgoing caregiver">
    ```
    Before you go â€” quick summary for handoff:
    
    [Auto-generated from today's data]
    
    Anything to add that Maria should know?
    ```
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Messages incoming caregiver">
    ```
    Handoff from Sarah:
    - Last meal: Lunch at 12:30 (ate well)
    - Meds: Morning dose confirmed, evening due at 8pm  
    - Mood: Alert and chatty this morning
    - Concerns: None today
    - Next up: Evening meds at 8pm
    ```
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Logs handoff in care plan">
    Records in Recent Updates with timestamp and both caregivers' names
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Example conversation:**
```
Sarah:  heading out, Maria is here
        
System: Before you go â€” quick summary for handoff:
        - Last meal: Lunch 12:30 (ate well)
        - Meds: Morning confirmed, evening at 8pm
        - Mood: Good today, alert
        - Concerns: None
        
        Anything to add that Maria should know?
        
Sarah:  she's been asking about her sister a lot today
        
System: Got it, thanks Sarah.
        
        [To Maria]
        Handoff from Sarah:
        - Last meal: Lunch 12:30 (ate well)  
        - Meds: Morning âœ“, evening due at 8pm
        - Mood: Alert and good today
        - Note: Asking about her sister frequently today
        - Next up: Evening meds at 8pm
```

### What a Good Handoff Includes

**Always:**
- Last meal (time and what)
- Medication status (confirmed or missed)
- Mood (alert, tired, confused, agitated, etc.)

**If applicable:**
- Any falls or incidents
- Bathroom assistance timing
- Pain level or pain medication given
- Visitors or phone calls

**Upcoming:**
- Next medication due time
- Appointments or scheduled visitors
- Specific tasks for incoming caregiver

**Context (the most valuable part):**
- "She's been asking about her sister today" - emotional context
- "He was frustrated this morning but settled down after breakfast"
- "She seems more tired than usual"

This qualitative information helps incoming caregiver understand what kind of day/shift to expect.

### Missed Handoff

If outgoing caregiver doesn't respond to handoff prompt:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Wait 15 minutes">
    Give them time - they might be driving home
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Compile automated summary">
    Use data logged throughout the day
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Send to incoming with note">
    "Auto-generated from today's logs (couldn't reach Sarah)"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Alert primary caregiver if pattern repeats">
    If same caregiver misses handoff 3+ times â†’ notify primary
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Rotating/New Staff Identity Verification

For professional caregivers (agency aides, rotating CNAs):

**First message from new phone number claiming to be staff:**

<Warning>
**Do NOT share any PHI until identity is confirmed.**
</Warning>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Verify identity">
    "Hi! Can you confirm the agency name and the client's name?"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check against care plan">
    If agency is documented in care plan AND name is correct â†’ proceed
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="If not documented, escalate">
    "I'll need to check with Sarah before sharing information. Just a moment."
    
    Immediately notify primary caregiver
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Once verified, proceed with filtered access">
    For known rotating staff (documented with phone numbers) â†’ normal handoff
    
    Access-level filtered per their documented role
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Example:**
```
[New number texts]: hi this is Maria from HomeHealth, starting shift

System: Hi Maria! Can you confirm the client's name?
        
Maria:  Mrs. Johnson
        
System: Thanks! [Proceeds with handoff]
        
[Or if suspicious]:

System: Thanks! Let me verify with the family. One moment.
        
        [To primary caregiver]:
        Someone claiming to be Maria from HomeHealth just texted. 
        Were you expecting a new aide today?
```

### Cold-Start Orientation for New Caregivers

When a new caregiver joins the care team for the first time:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Provide comprehensive orientation summary">
    Access-level filtered, includes:
    - Care recipient name, conditions, mobility status
    - Current medications + schedule (if schedule+meds access)
    - Daily routine and preferences
    - Emergency protocols customized to this family
    - Key things that work well ("She responds to music")
    - Things to avoid ("Don't mention the nursing home")
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Ask if they have questions">
    "Any questions before your shift starts?"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check in 1 hour into first shift">
    "How is everything going? Any questions?"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="End-of-first-shift: extra-detailed handoff prompt">
    Gather observations: "How did the shift go? Anything we should know for next time?"
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Example orientation:**
```
System: Welcome Maria! Quick orientation for Mrs. Johnson:
        
        - 78 years old, lives at home with daughter Sarah
        - Conditions: Heart failure, diabetes, early dementia
        - Mobility: Needs assistance standing/walking, uses walker
        - Medications: 3 times daily (I'll remind you when due)
        - Routine: Breakfast 8am, lunch 12pm, dinner 6pm
        - She loves talking about her garden and her grandkids
        - Please don't mention her late husband - it upsets her
        
        Emergency: If she has chest pain or trouble breathing, 
        call 911 immediately and text me. Address is 123 Oak St.
        
        Any questions before you start?
        
Maria:  What should I do if she refuses to eat?
        
System: Good question. Try offering smaller portions or her 
        favorite foods (she likes oatmeal and soup). If she 
        refuses multiple meals, let Sarah know. Don't force it.
        
Maria:  got it thanks
        
System: You're all set! I'll check in with you in an hour.
        
[1 hour later]
        
System: How's everything going, Maria?
```

## Integration with Other Protocols

Check-ins and handoffs work with:

- **Medication Management**: Reports medication compliance in daily summaries
- **Wellness Monitoring**: Passively collects wellness data from check-in responses
- **Care Schedules**: Agent schedules trigger check-ins at appropriate times
- **Provider Communication**: Identifies items to surface at appointments
- **Emergency Response**: Can trigger emergency protocol if check-in reveals concerning situation
